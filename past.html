Past

The history of the sewing machine is quite the story. With allegations of several inventors it is still undecided on who exactly is credited. The story begins in 1790 England with a man named Thomas Saint. He is noted as one of the first semi inventor because he had a patent for a needle that could poke through leather, however he did not have a machine designed to go along with his needle idea. Despite his attempts the actual idea of the machine and stitching did not come about until 1844 in the United States. Elias Howe patented the machine and began to try and sell the product to the world. However, the world was still far behind in technology and was not willing to accept the new concept. After some time passed, America finally caught on, and so did many other manufacturers including singer who still produces sewing machines today.
